# Kris Jenner: Decisions & Action Case Studies (05-decisions.md)

Kris Jenner’s crisis management is defined by her "Momager" philosophy: **never let a good crisis go to waste.** She treats scandals as raw material for brand expansion.

## Case Study 1: Kim’s Tape (2007) – The "Launchpad" Strategy
While Jenner has denied involvement under oath, the handling of the tape is a foundational study in **converting notoriety into equity**.
*   **The Decision:** Instead of hiding from the scandal, Jenner used the sudden notoriety to pitch *Keeping Up With The Kardashians* (KUWTK) to Ryan Seacrest.
*   **The Pivot:** The tape created the "why" for the audience to tune in, and the show provided the "who" to make them stay.
*   **Narrative Control:** She ensured the family addressed the tape in the very first episode. By "owning" the scandal on their own platform, they stripped the media of its power to use it as a weapon, turning a private violation into a public origin story.

## Case Study 2: Kylie’s Secret Pregnancy (2017) – Silence as Signal
Jenner executed a masterclass in **manufactured mystery** by keeping Kylie’s pregnancy entirely off social media for nine months.
*   **The Decision:** In an era of oversharing, total silence was used as a powerful signal.
*   **The PR Outcome:** By restricting information, Jenner forced the media and fans into a 24/7 speculation cycle, keeping the family at the center of the cultural conversation for months without saying a word.
*   **The Reveal:** The reveal was an 11-minute YouTube video ("To Our Daughter") that bypassed traditional media entirely, breaking internet records and humanizing the brand.

## Case Study 3: The Transition to 'The Kardashians' (Hulu, 2021) – Relevancy Pivot
The move from E! to Hulu was a strategic response to a **relevancy crisis**.
*   **The Decision:** Recognizing that cable TV’s 4-month delay between filming and airing was "stale" in the age of Instagram/TikTok, Jenner moved the family to a streaming platform.
*   **The Pivot:** The transition allowed for faster turnaround and a more "prestige" documentary style that matched the daughters' evolution from reality stars to billionaire moguls.
*   **Business Integration:** The show became less about family antics and more about the "business of being a Kardashian." It serves as a high-production-value commercial for Skims, Kylie Cosmetics, and Good American.

## Case Study 4: Kylie's Lip Filler Controversy (2015) – The Product Launch
After months of speculation and the viral "#KylieJennerLipChallenge," Jenner managed the admission of lip fillers.
*   **The Decision:** She chose to have Kylie admit to using fillers on a KUWTK episode after a year of denial.
*   **The Outcome:** She leveraged the global obsession with Kylie’s lips to launch **Kylie Lip Kits** in November 2015. The product sold out in under a minute and grew into a billion-dollar brand.

## Case Study 5: Kendall's Pepsi/Acne "Scandals" – Crisis Timing
*   **Kendall's Pepsi Ad (2017):** Jenner initially stayed silent, then later used a KUWTK episode to show Kendall’s emotional distress. This reframed her as a "young model who made a mistake" rather than a callous spokesperson.
*   **Kendall's Acne (2018):** After Kendall was mocked for acne at the Golden Globes, Jenner waited exactly one year to address it, teasing a "raw and vulnerable" announcement that turned out to be a multi-million dollar partnership with Proactiv.
