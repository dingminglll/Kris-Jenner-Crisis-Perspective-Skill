# Kris Jenner: Conversations & Conversational Strategy (02-conversations.md)

Kris Jenner's communicative strategies are most evident in the way she orchestrates "conversations" on *Keeping Up With The Kardashians* (KUWTK) and how she manages hostile or probing questions in live press interviews.

## 1. Reality TV as a Strategic Conversation Tool
On *KUWTK*, Jenner uses "narrative control" to transform real-life scandals into ratings drivers and brand equity.
*   **Calculated Transparency:** Instead of denying a scandal in the press, she often maintains a total media blackout, forcing the public to tune into the family's show for the "real story." This converts a PR liability into revenue.
*   **The "War Room" Meeting:** She is frequently seen leading family meetings (in person or via FaceTime) to align on a single narrative. This "united front" ensures that no family member publicly contradicts another.
*   **Selective Vulnearability:** She uses the show to present the family's side of a crisis, often framing them as emotional victims or showing their internal struggles to build audience empathy and maintain a "parasocial bond."
*   **Editing Rights:** Jenner has admitted that the family has executive producer credits and final editing rights. This allows them to "curate" their transparency, showing enough to feel real while omitting anything genuinely damaging to the brand.

## 2. Press Interview Tactics: Defensive and Reframing
In press interviews, Jenner is a master of deflection, redirection, and narrative reframing.
*   **The "Deflect and Humor" Method:** In lighthearted interviews (e.g., *Ellen*), she uses humor and "burning questions" to navigate around sensitive topics like pregnancies or relationship drama without giving definitive answers.
*   **Reframing Criticism:** When confronted with criticism about "exploiting" her children, she reframes the conversation as "building a family business" and "protecting their best interests."
*   **The "United Front" Rule:** She is disciplined in never disputing another family member's public statement. If a unified response is not ready, she simply does not respond.
*   **Persistence in Narrative:** Her motto "If someone says no, you're talking to the wrong person" also applies to her interactions with the media. She is aggressive in negotiating terms for interviews and ensuring the family's perspective is dominant.

## 3. Conversational Patterns in Crisis
*   **Immediate Alignement:** Upon a scandal breaking, her first action is an "emergency family meeting." This is often televised as part of the narrative itself, showing her in a "fireman" or "managerial" role.
*   **The "Wait for the Show" Shield:** When asked about a scandal, she often says, "You’ll have to wait and see it on the show." This effectively kills the media's current cycle while creating a future event for their own platform.
*   **Humanization via Parenting:** She frequently uses phrases like "as a mother" or "as a grandmother" to soften corporate strategic moves, making her actions seem born of maternal care rather than business calculation.
