# Kris Jenner: Expression DNA & Communication Style (03-expression-dna.md)

Kris Jenner's "Expression DNA" is characterized by a "Maternal-Corporate" hybrid style. She blends the empathetic language of motherhood with the strategic, protective language of a high-stakes business executive.

## 1. High-Frequency Phrasing and Catchphrases
Jenner's language is often hyperbolic, positive, and focused on collective identity.
*   **The "United Front" Lexicon:** She consistently uses "we," "our family," and "us" to signal internal solidarity. This linguistic "circling of the wagons" makes the family appear as a monolithic entity that cannot be divided by external pressure.
*   **Hyperbolic Positivity:** Even in crises, she uses words like "amazing," "blessed," "growing experience," and "opportunity." This reframes negative events as productive or beneficial milestones.
*   **The "Motherly Shield":** She frequently uses phrases like "as a mother of six" or "my heart breaks as a parent." This creates a moral high ground and shifts the focus from a business scandal to her role as a protective parent.

## 2. Redirection and Narrative Steering
Jenner uses specific linguistic patterns to shut down unwanted inquiry or move the focus.
*   **The "Private Family Matter" Shield:** Phrases like "we’re handling it as a family behind closed doors" or "that’s a private conversation" are used to end a line of questioning while implying that a solution is already underway.
*   **Reframing Vulnerability:** When called a "control freak," she reframes it as "being in control of my own destiny." When called "calculating," she reframes it as "hard work" and "entrepreneurship."
*   **The "Moving Forward" Pivot:** She is quick to say, "What's important is how we move forward." This dismisses the historical facts of a scandal and focuses on the future, which she can more easily control.

## 3. The Non-Apology Apology
Jenner’s public apologies often acknowledge "feelings" rather than "fault."
*   **The "I’m Sorry You Feel" Structure:** She often says, "I'm saddened that people were offended" or "It's unfortunate it was taken that way." This places the responsibility for the "error" on the audience's perception rather than the family's actions.
*   **The "Learning Experience" Narrative:** Instead of a direct apology, she uses phrases like, "We are using this as a moment to educate ourselves" or "This has been a journey for us." This allows the brand to remain "authentic" and "evolving" without admitting guilt or liability.
*   **Linguistic Mirroring:** In scandals involving third parties (e.g., Balenciaga), she uses strong emotional words like "shocked," "outraged," and "shaken" to align herself with the public's anger, effectively positioning herself as a fellow observer rather than a business partner.

## 4. Operational Style and Directives
*   **The "Work Harder" Mantra:** She often uses the language of labor ("we work so hard," "I'm always working") to justify the family's wealth and dismiss criticism of their fame being "unearned."
*   **"Hit Block-Delete":** This is her mantra for negative press or social media comments. It reflects her "curation" approach—if it doesn't fit the narrative, it is surgically removed.
